Tangible User Interfaces (TUIs)

Physical, non-digital objects that map to digital actions. They restore the satisfying tactile feel of analog controls while wielding immense digital power.

  • Smart Blocks: A child can change the room's music playlist simply by placing a specific wooden block on a shelf.
  • Analog Dials: Beautiful, weighty physical knobs placed on tables that allow the elderly to intuitively dial up the temperature or dim lights without navigating an app.

Micro-Gestures & Posture

The home uses mmWave radar not just to watch you, but to listen to your body language.

  • Intentional Gestures: A simple upward flick of the wrist in the air raises the music volume. A flat palm facing down instantly turns off all media in the room.
  • Postural Intent: Sitting down heavily on a reading chair automatically focuses a reading light over your shoulder.

Contextual Voice (No Wake Words)

We eliminate the robotic "Hey Siri" or "Alexa" commands. Interaction feels like talking to a human co-pilot in the room.

  • Gaze Tracking: Because the system knows where you are and what direction you are facing, you can simply look at the window blinds and say, "Close these," and the system understands the context.
Inclusivity

Designed for Everyone

A truly intelligent home adapts its interface to the user, not the other way around. By offering multiple input modalities, we ensure no one is left behind by technology.

The Generational Shift

A single screen-based app forces a 7-year-old, a 35-year-old, and an 80-year-old to use the exact same complex interface.

  • Children: Thrive on tactile play. TUIs (placing objects, touching surfaces) empower them to control their environment playfully.
  • Adults: Prefer speed and efficiency, utilizing micro-gestures and contextual voice while multitasking.
  • Elderly: Require familiarity and accessibility. High reliance on physical dials (TUIs) that mimic legacy analog switches, combined with conversational voice assistance.

The Edge Co-Pilot

The AI does not run the house; it assists you. It manages the mundane (parasitic power loads, baseline HVAC) while offering joyful, personalized suggestions.

The Context Graph as an Assistant

The local Edge AI aggregates spatial data (mmWave), biometric data (wearable heart rate), and UWB identity. Instead of autonomously forcing a change, it readies the environment. If it detects high stress, it might subtly dim the lights and prepare a calm playlist, waiting for a simple confirming nod (micro-gesture) or voice prompt from you to engage.

Privacy by Architecture

Because interaction relies heavily on biometrics and behavior, 100% of this processing happens on a local Edge node (e.g., Mac Mini M4). No cloud processing guarantees trust.

Commodity Empowerment

The intelligence lives in the software, allowing the use of cheap SLIP hardware (ESP32s, $5 radar modules) to democratize this high-end experience.

Output

Ambient Feedback

A truly interactive system must talk back. Without screens, the house communicates confirmations and states through the environment itself.

Light & Shadow

Baseboards gently pulse a soft blue to confirm a door has locked. "Follow-me" lighting softly illuminates the path ahead as you walk, acting as a silent dialogue between you and the space.

Haptic & Tactile Response

When you use a micro-gesture to turn down the volume, your smartwatch delivers a satisfying "click" vibration. The environment feels tactile and responsive, not floaty.

Auditory Sonification

Instead of harsh beeps, the home uses organic soundscapes. A pleasant, low-frequency chime confirms a command. Ultrasonic beaming directs a podcast specifically to your armchair.

Thermal Signaling

A localized warm radiant pulse from a floor tile or wall panel subtly signals that the system has recognized your presence and is adjusting the micro-climate to your preference.

Architecture Report

ARC Overview

The ARC (Adaptive Response Control) framework defines the methodology by which the Minimal Screen system co-creates the environment with the user, blending predictive intelligence with organic human inputs.

1. The Co-Pilot State Machine

Traditional smart home systems operate on a rigid boolean logic framework. The revised ARC framework operates as a probabilistic co-pilot. The system continuously calculates the probability of the user's current "State" based on a weighted matrix of inputs (UWB identity, mmWave posture, biometric stress), but it prioritizes Intentional Modalities (TUIs, Gestures, Voice) over autonomous assumptions.

For example, if the system predicts a "High-Energy/Stress" state upon a user's evening return, it prepares the environment (adjusting background HVAC, cueing a lighting scene) but waits for a soft confirmation—like the user placing their keys on a designated Tangible Smart Surface—to execute the full environmental shift, preserving user agency.

2. Modality Translation Layer

Data ingestion is highly specialized. Spatial vectors (mmWave) and physical object tracking (TUIs) are ingested continuously at 10Hz directly into the local Edge NPU to ensure physical interactions feel as instantaneous as flipping a traditional analog switch. The system achieves sub-50ms latency, critical for maintaining the illusion of a naturally responsive environment.

3. Actuation and Feedback Loops

A critical failure of purely autonomous environments is the lack of user feedback. ARC mandates a strict 1:1 feedback loop for all explicit interactions.

  • Ambient Affirmation: When a user executes a micro-gesture or voice command, the ARC engine triggers an immediate ambient response (e.g., a synchronized haptic pulse on a wearable and a subtle auditory chime) to confirm the system understood the intent.
  • Confidence Thresholds: Purely autonomous adjustments (like parasitic load shedding in empty rooms) are executed silently. However, state changes affecting human comfort require a 92% NPU prediction confidence or explicit human initiation via natural interfaces.

4. Edge-Local Privacy Assurance

To foster an environment where users feel comfortable utilizing natural body language and voice, all NLP (Natural Language Processing) and gesture recognition occurs on the local node. Raw acoustic data and mmWave point clouds are classified as Ephemeral Data, processed in volatile memory, and purged instantly. Only the structural "intent vectors" are retained for localized federated learning, ensuring the home remains a private sanctuary.
